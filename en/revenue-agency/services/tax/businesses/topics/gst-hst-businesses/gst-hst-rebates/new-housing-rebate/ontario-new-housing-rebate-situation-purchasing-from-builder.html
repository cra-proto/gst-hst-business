The Ontario enhanced new housing rebate (Ontario ENHR) provides for eligible individuals to recover up to $80,000 of the provincial portion of the HST paid on a new or substantially renovated home. The Ontario ENHR follows the eligibility conditions of the existing Ontario new housing rebate. This rebate is generally available where the agreement of purchase and sale for the home is entered into between the individual and the builder between April 1, 2026, and March 31, 2027.

The Ontario ENHR provides:

  • a full rebate (100% of the 8% provincial part of the HST) on a new home valued up to $1 million
  • a flat rebate of $80,000 for the provincial part of HST paid on a new home valued between $1 million and $1.5 million
  •  a partial rebate for the provincial part of HST paid on a new home valued between $1.5 million to $1.85 million

For a new home valued at $1.85 million and above, the existing Ontario new housing rebate would be available at a flat rate of $24,000.

Publications that will be updated include:

  • Form RC7190-ON, GST190 Ontario Rebate ScheduleFootnote * 
  • Guide RC4028, GST/HST New Housing Rebate

In addition to the Ontario ENHR, the province is providing additional relief equivalent to up to 5% of the federal part of the HST (5% Ontario top-up). To be eligible for the 5% Ontario top-up, individuals must be eligible for and have received the Ontario ENHR, either directly or through crediting by a builder. 

The updated form will include consent to share contact, banking, and rebate information with Ontario; A separate form is not required.

Buyers will be able to assign the Ontario ENHR and the 5% Ontario top-up to the builder once the forms are available.

Builders will be able to:

  • credit buyers the Ontario ENHR (8% provincial part of the HST) and 5% Ontario top-up at closing once the forms are available (by mid-July 2026);
  • complete their GST/HST return reporting GST/HST collected on the sale of the home and claims a deduction for having credited the 8% HST rebate to the buyer  - builders cannot claim a deduction on their GST/HST return for the 5% Ontario top-up relief, which is provided separately by Ontario
  • submit a completed GST190 that includes consent to share relevant contact, banking, and rebate information with the province of Ontario and associated RC7190 schedule; and
  • receive a separate payment from the province for the 5% Ontario top-up after the Ontario ENHR is assessed

Interaction with the First-time Home Buyers (FTHB) rebates

Where an individual is eligible for the federal FTHB GST rebate, the individual should proceed to claim that rebate first before the Ontario ENHR.  

Where an individual is eligible for both the Ontario ENHR and the Ontario FTHB rebate, the individual would be permitted to claim either or both Ontario rebates.

However, the total amount of all rebates for the 8% portion of the HST cannot exceed the lesser of $80,000 and the amount of 8% provincial portion of the HST payable in respect of the purchase or construction of the home.

This means the rebate cannot exceed the tax paid or payable, including any amount credited by a builder at the time of sale.
